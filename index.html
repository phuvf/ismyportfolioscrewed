Gross rent

This is the total rent you collect every month (or year).

Mortgage interest

The amount you pay each month (or year) in mortgage interest on your rental portfolio.

Don't include any payments that go towards paying off the loan amount.

If this is too complex, estimate your annual interest costs by multiplying the loan amount by the interest rate.

For example, a £ 100,000 loan at 5% interest rate:

£ 100,000 x 5% = £ 5,000 interest per year

Property operating costs

The amount you pay each month (or year) to keep your properties running.

This will include insurance, repairs, a gas safety certificate (you do have one, don't you?), accounting fees, travel expenses etc. etc.

Not sure how much you spend?

Shame on you! Get your books in order before HMRC do it for you.

In the meantime, a rough estimate would be around 10% of your gross rental income.

Gross income

The amount you earn in your job(s) outside of your property business, including any bonuses and commissions.

This should be the gross figure, before tax and NI are taken off.

None of these figures are collected - they only appear on your device. You're probably earning more than us anyway, so we'd only be jealous.

In the summer emergency budget (July 2015), George Osborne announced that tax relief on mortgage interest payments will be limited to 20% - as opposed to the current situation, where up to 45% tax relief can be claimed, depending on the rate of tax you currently pay.

These changes are going to be phased in over a period of four years, starting from April 2017.
